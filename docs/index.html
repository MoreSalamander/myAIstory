my-AI-story

Seed a world — characters, a theme, a loose direction — and my-AI-story writes a coherent serialized story, episode after episode, narrated as audio with a distinct voice for each character. Everything runs locally; no story leaves the machine.

Many episodes, one story. Continuity is the thread.

What it does

A series that remembers itself.

You give it a seed; it drafts a series bible — the canonical source of truth for characters, world facts, and the arc. Then it writes each episode against that bible and narrates it. The hard part of long-form generation isn't writing a page — it's writing page 200 without contradicting page 1. That's the problem my-AI-story is built to solve.

The sibling of my-AI-stro. Where my-AI-stro verifies knowledge, my-AI-story verifies narrative continuity — same Deterministic Scaffold thesis, new medium: human-seeded constraints → local-LLM synthesis → verification before anything is kept.

How an episode is made

Draft, verify against canon, narrate, update canon.
  1. Seed. Title, theme, characters, a loose plot direction, episode count and length.
  2. Series bible. A canonical source of truth is drafted — the world's facts, the cast, the arc.
  3. Draft + verify. A local LLM writes prose and speaker-tagged dialogue; it's checked against the bible before a word is kept — no quiet contradictions.
  4. Narrate. Verified episodes are rendered to audio with a per-character voice via a local TTS engine, then stitched into one narrated episode.
  5. Update canon. New facts — a death, a reveal, a new character — are fed back into the bible through a verified update, so the next episode still remembers this one.

Why it's built this way

Local-first, and verified — not vibes.

Synthesis runs on Ollama; voices on a local/open-source TTS engine; storage is the filesystem. No API keys to generate, nothing sent to the cloud. And continuity isn't left to the model's memory — it's a checkable artifact (the bible) the system holds the writing to, the same way its siblings hold code, video, or knowledge to a deterministic gate.
